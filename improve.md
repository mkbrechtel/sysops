Help us improve the library together! This is the place to learn how
the project works on the inside — coding conventions, contribution
flow, the release process, and the project's planning surface
(`issues/*.feature.md`, `issues/*.pattern.md`, `issues/*.bug.md`).

## Before you contribute — is this library for you?

This is an opinionated library. The values that drive it are:

- **Local-first** — your data, your services, on your boxes.
- **Agency at hand** — small, composable parts an operator can read,
  modify, replace, and own end-to-end.
- **Boring building blocks** over flashy platforms.
- **Cute over scaled** — small and medium teams in mind, not
  hyperscale.

Industry standards aren't bad on their own — many of them are how
they are because they solve real problems well. But *some* are
oversized, oversold, or actively at odds with the values above, and
those are the ones we call out in
[Anti-Patterns!](./anti-patterns.md). If your default position is
*"adopt the industry standard, whatever it is"* — Kubernetes for
everything, GitLab as the central hub, deployments via GitHub — your
feedback is genuinely appreciated, but the community here is
probably not a good fit for you. We'd rather sharpen our values than
dilute them.
